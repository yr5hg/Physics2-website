Chapter 27 Quiz

1. Two wires A and B with circular cross sections are made of the same metal and have equal lengths, but the resistance of wire A is three times greater than that of wire B.

(i) What is the ratio of the radius of A to B?

(ii) What is the ratio of the radius of A to that of B?.

2. A current-carrying cylindrical wire has a cross-sectional area that gradually becomes smaller from one end of the wire to the other. The current has the same value at all cross sections of the wire, so charge does not accumulate at any point.

(i) How does the drift speed vary along the wire as the area becomes smaller?

(ii) How does the electric field vary along the wire as the area becomes smaller?

3. A potential difference of 10.0 V is maintained across a 10.0 Ω resistor for a period of 20.0 s. What total charge passes by a point in one of the wires connected to the resistor in this time interval?

4. Three wires are made of copper having circular cross sections. Wire 1 has a length \( L \) and radius \( 2r \). Wire 2 has a length \( 2L \) and radius \( 3r \). Wire 3 has a length \( 2L \) and radius \( 5r \). Which wire has the smallest resistance?

5. A metal wire of resistance R is cut into three equal pieces that are then placed together side by side to form a new cable with a length equal to one-third the original length. What is the resistance of this new cable?

6. A metal wire has a resistance of 10.0 Ω at a temperature of \(20.0^\circ \mathrm{C}\). If the same wire has a resistance of 10.6 Ω at \(90.0^\circ \mathrm{C}\), what is the resistance of this wire when its temperature is \(220.0^\circ \mathrm{C}\)?

7. Two conductors made of the same material are connected across the same potential difference. Conductor A has twice the diameter and twice the length of conductor B. What is the ratio of the power delivered to A to the power delivered to B?

8. Two conducting wires A and B of the same length and radius are connected across the same potential difference. Conductor A has twice the resistivity of conductor B. What is the ratio of the power delivered to A to the power delivered to B?

9. Two lightbulbs both operate on \(120 \, \mathrm{V}\). One has a power of \(25 \, \mathrm{W}\) and the other \(100 \, \mathrm{W}\).

(i) Which lightbulb has higher resistance?

(ii) Which lightbulb carries more current when in use?

10. Wire B has twice the length and twice the radius of wire A. Both wires are made from the same material. If wire A has resistance \(R\), what is the resistance of wire B?
