Chapter 26: Capacitance and Dielectrics

Definition of Capacitance

Capacitance (\( C \)) is the ability of a system to store electric charge. It is defined as:

$$ C = \frac{Q}{\Delta V} $$

Where:

  • \( Q \): Charge on either conductor.
  • \( \Delta V \): Potential difference between the conductors.

Key points:

  • The SI unit of capacitance is the farad (F).
  • Common subunits: microfarad (\( \mu F \)) and picofarad (\( pF \)).
  • Capacitance depends on the physical properties of the capacitor, not the charge or voltage.

Calculating Capacitance

For specific configurations:

  • Parallel Plate Capacitor:

$$ C = \frac{\epsilon_0 A}{d} $$

Where:

  • \( \epsilon_0 \): Permittivity of free space.
  • \( A \): Plate area.
  • \( d \): Distance between plates.

Combinations of Capacitors

Capacitors can be combined in series or parallel:

  • In Parallel: The equivalent capacitance is the sum:

    • $$ C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2 + C_3 + \dots $$

  • In Series: The reciprocal of the equivalent capacitance is the sum of reciprocals:

    • $$ \frac{1}{C_{eq}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \frac{1}{C_3} + \dots $$

Energy Stored in a Capacitor

The energy (\( U \)) stored in a charged capacitor is given by:

$$ U = \frac{1}{2} C (\Delta V)^2 $$

The energy density in the electric field is:

$$ u_E = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 E^2 $$

Capacitors with Dielectrics

Inserting a dielectric between capacitor plates increases capacitance by a factor of the dielectric constant (\( \kappa \)):

$$ C = \kappa C_0 $$

Key points:

  • Dielectrics increase capacitance and maximum voltage before breakdown.
  • Examples include rubber, glass, and waxed paper.
