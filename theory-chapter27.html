Chapter 27: Current and Resistance

Electric Current

Electric current (\( I \)) is the rate of flow of electric charge. It is given by:

$$ I = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t} $$

Key points:

  • The SI unit of current is the ampere (A).
  • Conventional current flows in the direction of positive charges.
  • The relationship between drift velocity and current is:

    • $$ I = nqv_dA $$

Resistance

Resistance (\( R \)) is the opposition to the flow of current. It is given by Ohm's Law:

$$ R = \frac{\Delta V}{I} $$

Key points:

  • SI unit: ohms (\( \Omega \)), where \( 1 \Omega = 1 \text{V}/\text{A} \).
  • Resistivity (\( \rho \)) is a material property, related to resistance by:

    • $$ R = \rho \frac{L}{A} $$

  • Conductivity (\( \sigma \)) is the inverse of resistivity:

    • $$ \sigma = \frac{1}{\rho} $$

Temperature and Resistance

The resistance of a conductor changes with temperature. It is given by:

$$ R = R_0 [1 + \alpha (T - T_0)] $$

Key points:

  • \( R_0 \): Resistance at reference temperature (\( T_0 \)).
  • \( \alpha \): Temperature coefficient of resistivity.

Electrical Power

The power (\( P \)) delivered by a resistor is given by:

$$ P = I \Delta V $$

Alternative forms using Ohm's Law:

$$ P = I^2 R = \frac{(\Delta V)^2}{R} $$
